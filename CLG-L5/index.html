Bittersweet Quiz*

Some of us inhabit the bittersweet state instinctively, always have.

Whilst some of us avoid it as much as we can.

Some of us arrive there when we reach a certain age, or after facing life’s trials and triumphs.

If you’re wondering how inclined you are to this sensibility, you can take this quiz.

To find out how bittersweet you are, at this particular moment in time, indicate your level of agreement on a scale from 0 (not at all) to 10 (completely).

Question 1: Do you tear up easily at touching TV commercials?

Question 2: Are you especially moved by old photographs?

Question 3: Do you react intensely to music, art, or nature?

Question 4: Have others described you as an “old soul”?

Question 5: Do you find comfort or inspiration in a rainy day?

Question 6: Do you know what the author C. S. Lewis meant when he described joy as a “sharp, wonderful stab of longing”?

Question 7: Do you prefer poetry to sports (or maybe you find the poetry in sports)?

Question 8: Are you moved to goosebumps several times a day?

Question 9: Do you see “the tears in things”? (This phrase comes from Virgil’s Aeneid.)

Question 10: Do you feel elevated by sad music?

Question 11: Do you tend to see the happiness and sadness in things, all at once?

Question 12: Do you seek out beauty in your everyday life?

Question 13: Does the word poignant especially resonate with you?

Question 14: When you have conversations with close friends, are you drawn to talking about their past or current troubles?

Final Question: Do you feel the ecstatic is close at hand? (This may seem an odd question. But we're not talking about optimism, an easy smile; rather, the strange exaltation that yearning can bring.)

Legend to score range for answers

0-4: Not really

5: Neutral

6-10: Leaning towards yes

To score yourself on the Bittersweet Quiz, add up your responses and divide that total by 15.

If your number is lower than 3.8, you tend toward the sanguine.

If your number is above 3.8 and below 5.7, you tend to move between sanguine and bittersweet states.

If you score above 5.7, you’re a true connoisseur of the place where light and dark meet.

Exploratory studies show a high correlation between high scorers on the Bittersweet quiz and "high sensitivity", strong tendency to "absorption", and a moderate correlation with awe, self- transcendence, and spirituality.

*Bittersweet Quiz as developed by author Susan Cain, research scientist Dr. David Yaden, and cogntiive scientist, Dr. Scott Barry Kaufman
